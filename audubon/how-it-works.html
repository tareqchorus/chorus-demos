How It Works

From raw signals to editorial decisions

Chorus connects three data sources your team already has, layers audience intelligence on top, and delivers a daily content brief with ranked recommendations. The system learns from what your audience does next.

Data inputs

News monitoring

Global news, policy, and science coverage relevant to your mission areas. Filtered from the full firehose to your topic universe.

150K+ articles/day scanned

Member & supporter data

Your existing supporter records from Snowflake: engagement history, giving patterns, email response data, chapter affiliations, interest signals.

Your Snowflake warehouse

Web analytics

Page-level performance from audubon.org: article views, time on page, action-page click-through, donate-page click-through, bounce rates.

GA4 or equivalent
Chorus intelligence layer

Audience preference modeling

Per-supporter content profiles aggregated into segment portraits. Which topics, framings, and formats resonate with which parts of your audience, broken down by demographics and giving behavior.

Relevance scoring

Every incoming news story scored against your mission areas, current campaigns, and audience interest patterns. Stories ranked by how much of your audience they activate.

Performance prediction

Historical web and email performance data combined with audience profiles to predict which content will drive engagement, action-page visits, and donations from each segment.

Daily output

Content brief

Delivered each morning. Ranked stories with action recommendations (Amplify, Update, Gap, Skip). Each story includes audience segment portraits, web performance data for published articles, and editorial gap detection for stories you should cover but haven't yet. One page. Takes 5 minutes to read.

Team actions

Editorial decisions

Which stories your communications team should write about today, based on audience interest and mission alignment. Replaces guesswork with data.

Placement decisions

Which published articles to feature on your homepage, news page, and action pages. Prioritized by web performance and audience segment match.

Email campaign decisions

Which content to send to which segments, with framing and format recommendations tuned to each audience group's demonstrated preferences.

Results feed back
Learning loop

Engagement data feeds back into the model

Opens, clicks, donations, page views, action-page conversions, and time-on-page all feed back into the audience preference model and performance predictions. The system gets sharper over time. Recommendations in month 6 are meaningfully better than month 1.
